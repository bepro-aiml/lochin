5. DATA AVAILABILITY
Where does this data come from in Uzbekistan? What's hard to collect?
1.Data comes from Uzum app logs (clicks, views, purchases), user profiles, search history, and product database.
2.Hard to collect: accurate user demographics (age, gender), complete feedback (not everyone leaves reviews, ratings), 
and external factors like seasonality and trends. Paying in cash makes it difficult to know what the user is buying.


6. EVALUATION METRIC
How will you measure success? (Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1, AUC, etc.)

We chose Precision because in our problem personalized relevance is critical. 
The user sees a limited number of recommended products, and each should closely match their interests. 
Precision is better here because it focuses on the quality of recommendations (how accurate they are), rather than just the total number of relevant items found.

7. CLASS IMBALANCE
Is the target balanced or imbalanced? What's the distribution?

The distribution is 95% not purchased and 5% purchased.
The target is imbalanced: most cases are “not purchased” and only a small percentage are “purchased” (e.g., 95% vs 5%)

8. ETHICS & FAIRNESS
Who benefits? Who could be harmed? What needs human oversight?

Who benefits?
Uzum company — due to increased sales and revenue
Users — they receive personalized and convenient recommendations
Sellers — their products are more likely to be discovered by interested customers

Who could be harmed?
Users — if recommendations are too aggressive or lead to unnecessary spending
New or small sellers — their products may be shown less often
Users with inaccurate data — may receive irrelevant recommendations

What needs human oversight?
Checking recommendation quality (to ensure they are relevant and not harmful)
Monitoring bias (e.g., ensuring new sellers are not ignored)
Handling user complaints and adjusting the model
Controlling the use of personal data (privacy protection)


9. FAILURE MODES
What could go wrong? What are the consequences?

What could go wrong?
The model may recommend expensive products to low-income users
Users may receive irrelevant or random recommendations
The system may ignore new products or small sellers
There may be errors in data (incorrect user preferences)
Users may see identical or very similar products instead of variety

What are the consequences?
Users stop trusting recommendations and use the app less
Users may spend money on unnecessary products
Small sellers lose sales
Overall system performance decreases
User experience becomes boring due to lack of diversity
